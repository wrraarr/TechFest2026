Luddy TechFest 2026 · Micro:bit Exploration

Play it. Change it. Invent the next version.

This activity introduces physical computing through playful experiments with a micro:bit. Visitors press buttons, tilt, shake, and test ideas to make a shared system respond with sound, motion, and visuals. Then they take the next step: noticing what could work better, imagining improvements, and building their own version.

What participants do

1. Try the interaction Press a button, tilt the micro:bit, or give it a shake. Watch the room react on the big screen through music, characters, or motion drawing.
2. Notice what happens Ask what feels exciting, confusing, slow, surprising, or fun. Spot bugs, rough edges, and opportunities to make the experience clearer or more expressive.
3. Improve the idea Imagine a better control, a new response, a more playful animation, or a smarter controller. Sketch a fix or invent a fresh feature.
4. Build the next version Use MakeCode, HTML, or another simple prototype to program a controller, remix a page, or vibe code a new interactive experiment.

What this teaches

  • Computers can respond to real-world input like motion, buttons, light, and touch.
  • Interactive systems are designed, tested, and improved by observing real people use them.
  • Coding is not just writing instructions. It also includes noticing problems, inventing better ideas, and shaping how something feels.
  • One small controller can become music, animation, a tool, or an entirely new web page.

Prompt to post beside the activity

Can you make the system do something new?

Test one interaction. Find one thing that could be better. Invent one fix, feature, or surprise. Then sketch or code your own version.
